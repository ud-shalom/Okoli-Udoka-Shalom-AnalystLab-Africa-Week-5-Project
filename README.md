# Excel Bank: Campaign Performance & Customer Financial Analysis
## Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of bank marketing campaign performance and customer financial behavior. By analyzing customer demographics, loan distributions, and campaign engagement, the project identifies key drivers for term deposit subscriptions and evaluates financial health across different customer segments.

## Tools Used
SQL (Microsoft SQL Server Management Studio): Utilized for data cleaning, structural exploration, and initial data validation (checking record counts and column structure).

Excel: Used for advanced data analysis, creation of interactive dashboards, and visualization of key performance indicators (KPIs).

## Data Cleaning & Preparation
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): Used SQL queries to confirm the integrity of the dbo.bank dataset, specifically:

Verified total row count (11,162) and column count (17).

Identified data quality issues, such as 'unknown' entries in the 'job' category (70 instances).

Data Transformation: Organized and cleaned the data in Excel to prepare it for PivotTable aggregation, ensuring accurate representation of customer demographics and campaign outcomes.

## Key Insights
### 1. Campaign Performance
High Conversion: Achieved a 47.38% subscription rate (5,289 subscribers).

Engagement Trends: May emerged as the peak month for subscription activity.

Communication Effectiveness: Telephone contact is the most effective channel, yielding the longest average engagement duration.

Data Anomaly: Identified a tracking concentration in the "Campaign Days Analysis," suggesting a potential system logging error.

### 2. Customer Financial Analysis
Demographic Skew: The customer base is heavily concentrated in the 60+ age group.

Loan Preference: Housing loans are significantly more prevalent than personal loans.

Default Correlation: Customers in default exhibit a negative average account balance (-61.80), indicating a direct correlation between defaulting status and overall financial instability.

Job Distribution: "Management" is the most prominent job category among customers.

## Recommendations
Align Campaigns: Schedule high-impact marketing to coincide with seasonal peaks observed in May.

Optimize Outreach: Focus on telephone-based engagement to maximize duration and conversion.

Mitigate Default Risk: Launch proactive financial support programs for customers currently in default.

Diversify Lending: Increase promotion of personal loans to balance credit product distribution.

Improve Data Quality: Resolve tracking/logging errors identified in campaign data to ensure better accuracy in future reporting.

## Conclusion
The analysis highlights that while marketing campaigns successfully reach a large portion of the customer base, there are clear opportunities to optimize reach through better timing and channel selection. Addressing the financial vulnerability of defaulting customers and diversifying loan offerings remain critical for long-term customer stability and bank growth.
